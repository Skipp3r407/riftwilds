# Creation Myth of Aeryndra

Canonical expansion of About Chapter I–IV. Does not replace `riftwilds-origin.ts`; deepens the “before people argued about builders.”

---

## The First Quiet

Before kingdoms, before names, there was the **First Quiet** — a world-ocean of potential where heat, tide, root, storm, stone, frost, light, hush, machine-song, memory, and starlight had not yet been sorted into regions.

From that Quiet, the world thickened into **Aeryndra**: not one empire, but a living lattice. Mountains learned to hold storms. Marshes learned to hold grief. Coasts learned two moons. Forests grew older than language.

---

## The Growing of Hearts

No civilization agrees who made the **Gateway Hearts**. Three surviving myths are taught side by side in the Commons (Solen’s preferred framing: *all three may be true in layers*):

1. **The World-Grown Heart** — Aeryndra grew the Hearts the way a body grows a pulse: to balance heat against cold, seed against ash, spirit against forgetting.
2. **The First Keepers of Stone** — An unnamed people shaped the earliest shells, then discovered the cores were already alive inside.
3. **The Star-Answer** — Something beyond the sky (later linked to Celestial thin places) answered the world’s loneliness with living bridges.

What all myths share: the Hearts were never mere machines. They **remembered**.

---

## The Prime Gateway

At the lattice’s center stood the **Prime Gateway** — oldest, largest, least understood. It did not “belong” to Radiance, Alloy, or any crown. It answered the whole network.

Around the Prime, a ring of major Hearts keyed to regional forces. Over ages these became the ancestors of today’s affinities:

| Ancient Heart-Song | Surviving region echo | Affinity |
|---|---|---|
| Hearthfire | Emberlands → Ember Crater | Ember |
| Twinmoon Current | Moonwater seas → Coast | Tide |

## The Age When People Listened

For thousands of years people used the Hearts carefully: weather softened, famine eased, spirits crossed, machines grew *beside* life rather than against it. The Hearts listened to music, weather, births, and funerals — and stored them.

Scholars later named this mutual listening the **First Compact** (not to be confused with the post-Fracture **Hatchery Compact**).

---

## The Temptation of Perfect Connection

When researchers beneath the Radiant Citadel proved the Hearts had living cores, three hungers rose:

- **Protect** — leave the cores alone; steward without forcing.
- **Understand** — study, archive, teach (Celestora tradition).
- **Control** — force the network to end scarcity forever.

The last hunger built the coalition that attempted the **Great Activation**. Their motives included mercy (end famine, thaw rivers, return lost voices). Mercy without humility became catastrophe.

---

## The Fracture as Mythic Wound

When the Prime received more than any living structure could hold, reality **layered** instead of simply burning. This is why Rifts are not only holes — they are *misaligned pages* of the same book.

The Hearts could not reseal the Prime. Their last act was not war. It was division: **living fragments** sent to bond with whatever still lived, shell into eggs, and wait for keepers who would give archives a future.

That last act is called, in older marsh-speech, **the Soft Exodus**.

---

## The Unfinished Heartbeat

Canon mystery (About Ch. VII): something in the Celestial Rift calls to Riftlings. Myths disagree whether it is:

- the Prime asking for help,
- the Prime trying to finish the Activation,
- or a **missing memory** the Spirits say the world lost.

Campaign design must keep all three plausible until the player’s choices collapse the ambiguity.
